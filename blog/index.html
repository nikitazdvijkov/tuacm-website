POSIX (Mac and Linux)


The Portable Operating System Interface (POSIX) refers to a group of IEEE standards for making programs work consistently between Unix-based operating systems, which include Linux and MacOS but not Windows. Articles in this section consist of things you can do both on Mac and Linux.
